Copenhagen Waste-to-Energy Plant

Amager Bakke in Denmark, a modern plant that converts 400,000 tons of waste into energy

  • Annual Electricity: 63,000 homes
  • Annual Heating: 160,000 homes
  • CO2 Savings: 100,000 tons/year

Singapore Tuas WTE Facility

Singapore's largest waste to energy facility with a processing capacity of 3,000 tons/day

  • Power Generation: 22 megawatts
  • 90% reduction in landfill space
  • Supplies a total of 2,500 homes

Circular Economy Approach

How waste to energy fits into the broader concept of circular economy

Sustainable Resource Management

Waste to energy technology plays a crucial role in the circular economy by recovering energy from materials that cannot be efficiently recycled, creating a closed-loop system that maximizes resource utilization and minimizes environmental impact.

Benefits of Integration

  • Reduces landfill dependency
  • Recovers energy from non-recyclable waste
  • Lowers greenhouse gas emissions
  • Creates valuable by-products like ash for construction
  • Supports renewable energy targets
